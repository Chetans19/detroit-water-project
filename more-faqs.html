Who created the Detroit Water Project?

Are either of you based in Detroit?
No. Tiffani Bell is currently in Oakland, CA and Kristy Tillman is based in Boston, MA.


Is Detroit Water Project affiliated with the Detroit Water and Sewage Department (DWSD)?
No. Our only connection with the Detroit Water and Sewage Department is making sure donors have the correct information they need to make a payment for a resident through the DWSD website.


What was the main influence for starting this initiative?
We wanted a way to immediately provide relief to Detroit residents. We'd seen a lot of organizations and a lot of crowdfunding campaigns but we wanted a way to move more swiftly than that.

Generally with crowdfunding people have to wait until the entire fund is raised, and we were not satisfied with that model. We also wanted to ensure our donations went directly to the residents, and that was not a guarantee through any of the campaigns we saw going on at the time.


How many donors have pledged?
As of August 1, 2014, over 7,000 donors have pledged.


Why did you decide to go with the anonymous donor route for those who choose to pay others' bills?
It is the utmost importance to us to keep as much information as private as possible on both ends of the transaction. We figured that people just wanted to help or just needed help.

The logistics of our current system would also make it difficult to swap information between donors and residents; it would require an extra step in a process that we are trying to expedite as swiftly as possible.


Will the Detroit Water Project site stay up for a certain period of time?
We have no time frame on when we'll sunset the project. We are committed to keeping it up as long as it's useful to both donors and residents. Tiffani and a team of volunteers are in the process of automating some of our processes that we are currently handling manually to make it much easier to maintain as we move forward.
